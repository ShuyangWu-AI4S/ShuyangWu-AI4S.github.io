### 1. Physics-Informed Machine Learning & Operator Learning
This research direction focuses on deeply analyzing the convergence mechanisms of Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) and Neural Operators (e.g., FNO, PINO) in continuous spaces. It aims to reconstruct automatic differentiation gradient flows and introduce spatiotemporal causality weights to resolve the long-standing challenges of gradient stiffness imbalances and numerical distortions in solving inverse problems for highly non-linear partial differential equations (PDEs).

### 2. Spatiotemporal Representation Learning for Fiber Optic Sensing
This area concentrates on feature extraction and physics-grounded denoising of high-frequency raw demodulated signals from Distributed Acoustic Sensing (DAS). It explores how to strictly embed wave optics equations (e.g., Green's function masks) and scattering statistical priors (e.g., Rayleigh/Rician distributions) into the self-attention mechanisms of deep learning frameworks, enabling high-fidelity reconstruction of physical field characteristics from noisy spatiotemporal waterfall plots plagued by algorithmic distortions.

### 3. Inverse Problems in Fluid Dynamics & Digital Twins
This direction is dedicated to the engineering implementation of non-invasive inverse solving, mapping 1D sensing signals back to 3D continuous fluid fields. Targeting complex pipe network conditions with multi-phase flows involving high Reynolds numbers, transient shock waves, and cavitation, this research builds high-real-time, zero-shot generalizable digital twin foundations to achieve spatial boundary assimilation and transient flow regime inversion of fluid governing equations.

### 4. AI4S for Multiphase Chemically Reacting Flows
*(Reserved as a long-term exploratory direction for Year 2 and beyond)*  
Leveraging a cross-disciplinary background in energy thermal physics, this direction explores the integration interfaces between data-driven surrogate models and physics-informed neural solvers under multiphase chemically reacting flow conditions. The ultimate goal is to provide full-stack intelligent monitoring solutions for zero-carbon fuel pipe networks.
